Source. Every roster, club, position, birth date, height, cap and goal figure is taken verbatim from the official FIFA squad lists released 24 June 2026; ages are as of the tournament. Players are grouped by position (GK → DF → MF → FW) and sorted by shirt number within each position. The coloured tags flag each squad's most-capped player, top scorer and youngest player. Squad facts (average age, players based abroad, etc.) are computed from that same data.

Player ratings. The OVR chip on each player is the EA Sports FC 26 overall rating (launch database, updated 19 Sep 2025), matched to the squad lists by name and date of birth. A gold ★ Key badge marks the highest-rated player in each position line — the goalkeeper, defender, midfielder and attacker most worth watching — and a green bubble shows a young player's growth potential. Ratings exist for 701 of 832 players; some squads built around domestic leagues (e.g. South Africa, Egypt) are only partly covered, so players without an EA rating show a dashed “–” rather than a guessed number. Ratings are EA's and are shown for reference only.

Squad profile. Each open squad shows a strengths & weaknesses read across its four lines — goalkeeping, defense, midfield and attack. A line's rating is the average OVR of the players that would actually fill it (best goalkeeper, top four defenders, top four midfielders, top three attackers), then ranked against the other qualified teams. A line in the top quarter of the field is flagged as a strength (green); the bottom quarter is a weakness (red); the rest sit mid-table (blue). Hover any bar for its exact rating and field rank. Lines with no rated players show a dashed “–” and are left out of the summary.

Round of 32 — June 28 to July 3. The 32-team bracket is complete: 12 group winners, 12 runners-up and the eight best third-placed teams. It's a fixed bracket with no redraws — every nation already knows its full path to the final. Opening ties include South Africa–Canada, Brazil–Japan, Netherlands–Morocco, Spain–Austria, Portugal–Croatia and Argentina–Cabo Verde.
